# Rodrigo França

Graduate student in Computational Analysis and Public Policy at the University of Chicago, where I try to bring together the rigor of econometrics and the tools of modern data science. I'm drawn to questions where getting the causal story right actually matters — urban policy, public health, crime, housing.

Incoming research intern at the World Bank's DIME division (Summer 2026). Previously research assistant at the Mansueto Institute for Urban Innovation and at the Insper Cities Lab in São Paulo, where I spent two years doing empirical urban and crime economics research, mentoring students, and teaching applied econometrics and GIS.

## what I work on

- **Causal inference** — DiD, RDD, IV, staggered designs. Published in *Utilities Policy*; working paper on police body cameras forthcoming.
- **Machine learning at scale** — end-to-end pipelines, feature engineering, gradient boosting. Built a classifier across 1.1M NYC properties.
- **Spatial econometrics and urban economics** — housing markets, crime, public health, infrastructure.
